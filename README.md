# FloodScope Dashboard

## Project Overview
FloodScope is a building-level probabilistic flood risk screening dashboard developed as part of an industry-focused climate risk analytics project across the Greater Toronto Area (GTA).

The project analyses flood exposure at the building level to support risk assessment, geographic analysis, and data-driven decision support through interactive visualisation and dashboarding.

---

## Business Problem
Organisations and lending institutions face increasing exposure to climate-related risks, particularly flooding events that may impact infrastructure, assets, and operational planning.

This project was designed to:
- identify high-risk buildings,
- analyse regional flood exposure trends,
- support portfolio-level risk monitoring,
- and communicate insights through interactive dashboarding.

---

## Dataset
The analysis was conducted using large-scale building-level flood exposure data covering more than 592,000 buildings across the Greater Toronto Area.

Key variables included:
- exceedance probabilities,
- Monte Carlo risk metrics,
- geographic segmentation,
- and flood classification indicators.

---

## Key Insights
- Approximately 11% of analysed buildings showed greater than 50% flood exceedance probability.
- Toronto accounted for a significant concentration of high-risk properties within the analysed region.
- The dashboard enabled interactive exploration of regional flood exposure patterns and risk segmentation.

---

## Dashboard Features
- Interactive flood risk visualisation
- Building-level filtering and analysis
- Regional exposure comparison
- Risk classification summaries
- Portfolio-level monitoring interface

---

## Business Impact
The project demonstrates how analytics and dashboarding can support organisations in identifying climate-related flood exposure and improving data-driven decision-making.

The solution translates complex probabilistic modelling outputs into accessible visual insights for operational and strategic stakeholders.
